### Key Differences from Study Mode

**No grading buttons** - Just view and swipe
**No card actions** - Can't edit, delete, or add cards
**Definition always revealed** - No need to tap to show answer
**Shows all cards** - Not filtered by due date
**Minimal toolbar** - Just a back button

## Navigation

### Swiping Between Cards

- **Swipe left** - Next card
- **Swipe right** - Previous card
- **Smooth animations** - Easy on the eyes
- **Loop to beginning** - Swiping past the last card goes back to first

### Card Counter

*(If enabled)*
- Shows current position (e.g., "Card 5 of 42")
- Helps track progress through deck
- Located at top of screen

### Exiting Browse Mode

- **Tap back arrow** (top-left)
- **Device back button**
- Returns to previous screen (deck menu or cards list)

---

## Interactive Features

Even though you can't grade cards, Browse Mode still supports:

### Pinch to Zoom

**Gesture:** Pinch out/in on any card section

**Use case:**
- Read small text
- Examine image details
- Adjust for readability

Zoom level persists across cards in the session.

### Adjust Term/Definition Ratio

**Gesture:** Drag the horizontal bar between sections

**Use case:**
- Optimize screen space
- Show more of longer content
- Match your reading preference

Ratio persists across cards in the session.

### HTML Content Support

Browse Mode fully renders:
- **Bold/italic text** (`<b>`, `<i>`, `<strong>`, `<em>`)
- **Images** (`<img>` tags)
- **Links** (clickable)
- **Lists** (`<ul>`, `<ol>`)

See [Cards List - Formatting](/docs/cards-list/formatting/) for HTML details.

## Tips

### Use for Initial Review
- Browse all cards in a new deck before your first study session
- Helps gauge difficulty and time needed
- Mentally prepare for what's coming

### Check Before Sharing
- Always browse your deck before sharing the database file
- Catch typos, incomplete cards, or sensitive information
- Make a good impression on recipients

### Combine with Cards List
- Browse Mode for quick visual scanning
- Cards List for finding, editing, organizing
- Each has its strengths

### Don't Overthink It
- Browse Mode is simple by design
- Just view and swipe
- No pressure, no tracking, no consequences
